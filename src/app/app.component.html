The Eyes Don't Lie

Creating technology to empower law enforcement in their prosecution of impaired driving

The Problem

With the recent legalization of recreational marijuana, law enforcement agencies have been left a gap in determining THC levels for safe driving. How much, when, and the level of insobriety from THC consumption cannot be determined with any level of reliability by any technology in the field today.

The current methods to detect marijuana consumption require laboratory analysis and still are unable to give an accurate level of sobriety.

Numerous studies have shown that a blood concentration (or any bodily fluid concentration) of THC does not correlate with sobriety.

The Solution

We believe optolyzers are the future of sobriety detection. Instead of looking for drugs, we’re looking for impairment.

The eyes provide signs of drug use that can’t be compensated for by weight, age, or drug tolerance.

Technology

Our optolyzer uses cutting edge optics and high speed cameras to capture the eye under duress.

We provide stimulus to the eye and conduct the same tests that Drug Recognition Experts use today, plus much more.

Our proprietary solving techniques use AI modeling to determine the drug classification and the subject’s relative impairment to that of a 0.08 BAC.
